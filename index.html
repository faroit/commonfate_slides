Common Fate Theory

  • Bregman in 1994 used term "Common Fate" in auditory scene analysis.
  • He describes common fate as the ability to group sound objects based on their common motion over time
  • Humans ability to detect and group sound sources by small differences in the FM and AM modulation is outstanding

Related Work

  • Frequency- dependent activation matrices by using a source/filter-based model (Hennequin et. al. 2011)
  • HR-NMF models each complex entry of a time-frequency as a linear combination of its neighbours (Badeau et. al. 2011)
  • Exploiting AM by computing a modulation spectrogram and factorise using NTF (Barker et. al. 2013)

Common Fate Transform

Common Fate Transform

  • STFT transforms signal into $X(\omega, \tau) \in \mathbb{C}$
  • $X$ is split into overlapping rectangular $(N_a \times N_b)$ patches
  • Compute complex 2D-DFT of each patch
  • Yields in an 4D-Tensor $\mathbf{V}^{N_a \times N_b \times N_f \times N_t}$
  • $a \rightarrow$ modulation texture (spaning frequency bins)
  • $b \rightarrow$ modulation texture (spaning time frames)
  • $f \rightarrow$ frequency index of modulation texture on t-f plane
  • $t \rightarrow$ time index of modulation texture on t-f plane

Common Fate Transform

In Detail

Compared to modulation spectrograms...

  • CFT is computed using complex STFT $X$
    • Easily invertible
    • Models phase dependencies between neighbouring STFT entries
  • Patches span/merge several frequency bins
  • Results in modulation texture

Common Fate Model

NMF

$$\sum\limits_{j=1}^{J} \mathbf{w}_{j}(f) \circ \mathbf{h}_{j}(t) $$

CPD/PARAFAC/NTF

$$\sum\limits_{j=1}^{J} \mathbf{w}_{j}(f) \circ \mathbf{m}_{j}(b) \circ \mathbf{h}_{j}(t)$$

Common Fate Model

$$\sum\limits_{j=1}^{J} \mathbf{P}_{j}(a,b,f) \circ \mathbf{h}_{j}(t)$$

Signal Separation

  1. Compute the CFT from audio signal to get tensor $\mathbf{V}$
  2. Initialise $\mathbf{P}$ and $\mathbf{h}$ with random non-negative values
  3. Apply multiplicative update rule to minimize $\beta$-divergence
  4. Synthesise factorised components using Wiener filtering
  5. Inverse CFT

Conclusion

  • CFT a transformation based on a complex tensor representation computed from patches of the STFT
  • CFM derived from the idea of humans perceiving common modulation over time as one source.
  • Our results on unisonous musical instruments indicate that this method can perform well for this scenario.
